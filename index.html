Sea otters play an essential role in the food web and keep important elements of coastal ecosystems like kelp forests and seagrass beds in balance. They spend most of their time in the water, but sometimes come onto land to sleep or rest. They like to lay on their backs in water to relax and sleep.

Monterey Bay Aquarium Live Sea Otter Cam

Sea Otter diets mostly consist of invertebrates like crabs, snails, urchins, clams, abalone, and mussels and fish. They grab a rock from the bottom of the seafloor and crack open clamshells with it to eat.
